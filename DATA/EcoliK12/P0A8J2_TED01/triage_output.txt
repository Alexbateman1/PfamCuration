CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in the restart of stalled replication forks, which
CC       reloads the DnaB replicative helicase on sites other than the origin of
CC       replication (PubMed:8663104, PubMed:8663105, PubMed:8663106). Functions
CC       in the PriA-PriB and PriA-PriC restart pathways, but probably not in
CC       the PriC-Rep pathway (PubMed:15238512). Displaces ssDNA from a PriB-
CC       ssDNA complex (PubMed:25265331, PubMed:30659961). Also involved in
CC       inducing stable DNA replication during SOS response. It forms, in
CC       concert with DnaC protein and other prepriming proteins DnaB, PriA,
CC       PriB and PriC a prepriming protein complex on the specific site of the
CC       template DNA recognized by PriA (PubMed:8663104, PubMed:8663105). Binds
CC       single-stranded (ss)DNA in a cooperative manner; both the N- and C-
CC       terminal domains are required for cooperativity (PubMed:15238512,
CC       PubMed:25053836). Forms a spiral filament on ssDNA (PubMed:25053836).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15238512, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25053836,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:30659961, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8663104,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8663105, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8663106}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homooligomerizes; monomer, dimer, trimer, tetramer and
CC       pentamer have been seen (PubMed:6453123). Exists in a monomer:trimer
CC       equilibrium (PubMed:23418648). During primosome assembly PriA binds to
CC       replication forks, subsequently PriB then DnaT bind in a 'hand-off'
CC       mechanism, which allows DnaC to load helicase DnaB onto the fork
CC       (PubMed:8663104, PubMed:8663105, PubMed:8663106, PubMed:17588514).
CC       Component of the replication restart primosome, which is composed of
CC       PriA, PriB, PriC, DnaB and DnaT; DnaG primase associates transiently
CC       with this complex (PubMed:6454139, PubMed:8663104, PubMed:8663105,
CC       PubMed:17588514). Very high levels of DnaT in vitro bypass PriB in
CC       primase activity (PubMed:8663106). Weakly binds PriB; binding increases
CC       in the presence of ssDNA; as DnaT levels increase PriB dissociates from
CC       ssDNA (PubMed:17588514, PubMed:25265331, PubMed:30659961).
