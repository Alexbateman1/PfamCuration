CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as a zinc chaperone in the AztABCD zinc transport system
CC       (PubMed:26468286, PubMed:30353723, PubMed:31428696, PubMed:32781785).
CC       Directly transfers one zinc cation to the solute binding protein AztC;
CC       the transfer occurs without the formation of a stable interaction
CC       (PubMed:26468286, PubMed:31428696, PubMed:32781785). Binds 3 Zn(2+),
CC       two with high affinity and one with low affinity, and transfers only
CC       Zn(2+) bound to site 2 to AztC (PubMed:26468286, PubMed:31428696).
CC       Likely functions to store zinc in the periplasm and may be important
CC       for zinc accumulation in zinc-limited environments (PubMed:32781785).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26468286, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30353723,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31428696, ECO:0000269|PubMed:32781785}.

CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Periplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26468286,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31428696}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Up-regulated upon zinc starvation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26468286}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: No effect on growth and no effect on zinc
CC       cellular level in zinc repleted media (PubMed:30353723). In zinc
CC       limited media, growth is reduced in a znuA mutant background and also
CC       in an aztC mutant background (PubMed:30353723). In zinc depleted media,
CC       zinc cellular levels are reduced and levels are further reduced in a
CC       znuA mutant background, an aztC mutant background or a znuA and aztC
CC       mutant background (PubMed:30353723). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30353723}.
