CC   -!- FUNCTION: McdA and McdB together mediate carboxysome (Cb) spacing,
CC       size, ultrastructure and probably inheritance in the cell
CC       (PubMed:30520729, PubMed:34340540, PubMed:34406783). Together they
CC       prevent Cb aggregation (PubMed:34340540, PubMed:34406783). McdA is an
CC       ATPase that forms dynamic gradients on the nucleoid in response to
CC       adapter protein McdB, which associates with carboxysomes
CC       (PubMed:30520729, PubMed:34406783). The interplay between McdA
CC       gradients on the nucleoid and McdB-bound carboxysomes result in the
CC       equal spacing of Cbs along the cell length (PubMed:30520729,
CC       PubMed:34406783). McdB may have an additional function in cell divison
CC       (PubMed:30520729, PubMed:34340540, PubMed:34406783). Stimulates the
CC       ATPase activity of McdA, causing McdA to be released from DNA.
CC       Overexpression leads to loss of McdA oscillation and formation of large
CC       Cb aggregates which colocalize with McdB, as well as diffuse McdB
CC       staining in the cytoplasm (PubMed:30520729). Undergoes liquid-liquid
CC       phase separation between pH 6.5-7.5 and at concentrations between 1 uM
CC       and 167 uM (PubMed:31899489, PubMed:37668016). Forms polar foci upon
CC       overexpression in E.coli (PubMed:30520729, PubMed:31899489,
CC       PubMed:34406783, PubMed:37668016). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30520729,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:31899489, ECO:0000269|PubMed:34340540,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:34406783, ECO:0000269|PubMed:37668016}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Incorrect positioning (aggregation) of carboxysomes results
CC       in reduced CO(2) fixation by encapsulated RuBisCO, which leads to
CC       slower growth, cell elongation, asymmetric cell division and an
CC       increase in RuBisCO levels (PubMed:34340540).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:34340540}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Self-associates, interacts with McdA probably via the C-
CC       terminus of both proteins (PubMed:30520729, PubMed:34406783).
CC       Homohexamerizes (PubMed:33638215, PubMed:37668016). Probably a trimer
CC       of dimers (Probable) (PubMed:37668016). Interacts with most of the
CC       shell components of the carboxysome (CcmK2, CcmK3, CcmK4, CcmL and
CC       CcmO, but not CcmP) via its C-terminus (PubMed:30520729,
CC       PubMed:33638215, PubMed:34406783, PubMed:37668016).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30520729, ECO:0000269|PubMed:33638215,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:34406783, ECO:0000269|PubMed:37668016,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:37668016}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Carboxysome {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30520729,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:34406783}. Note=In the absence of carboxysomes (a
CC       ccmK2LMNO operon deletion) this protein is found diffused in the
CC       cytoplasm (PubMed:30520729). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30520729}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Transcription is reproducibly circadian, under control of
CC       RpaA (PubMed:24315105). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24315105}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The N-terminus (approximately residues 1-18) is intrinsically
CC       disordered, and not necessary for hexamerization; its net positive
CC       charge modulates liquid-liquid phase separation (replacing Arg or Lys
CC       residues with polar Gln decreases phase separation). The central domain
CC       has a stable alpha-helical structure (approximately residues 19-121),
CC       dimerizes and drives phase separation. The C-terminal domain
CC       (approximately residues 122-152) probably has 2 alpha-helices and is
CC       responsible for hexamerization (PubMed:37668016).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:37668016}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Loss of McdA oscillation, carboxysomes (Cb) have
CC       much larger size variation and cluster, forming irregularly-shaped foci
CC       mostly near the poles, with smaller foci elsewhere (Cbs are usually
CC       regularly spaced along the central axis of the cell) (PubMed:30520729).
CC       Double mcdA-mcdB deletions have a similar phenotype (32 degrees Celsis,
CC       80 umol photon m(-2) sec(-1) constant light, 2% CO(2))
CC       (PubMed:30520729). At 20, 30 or 40 degrees Celsius (60 umol photon m(-
CC       2) sec(-1) constant light, 2% CO(2)) Cbs lose regular spacing and
CC       instead form large aggregates, increased level of RuBisCO at 20 and 30
CC       degrees. In mcdB deletions cell division is asymmetric at all 3
CC       temperatures. Slower growth at high CO(2) but not in ambient air at 30
CC       or 40 degrees Celsius, slower growth in high CO(2) or ambient air at 20
CC       degrees Celsius (PubMed:34340540). This strain was isolated from
CC       freshwater that varies from 8 to 25 degrees Celsius annually.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30520729}.
