CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays a role in carbon catabolite repression (CCR), which
CC       prevents expression of genes involved in catabolism of nonpreferred
CC       carbon sources when glucose is present. Encoded in dual-function Spot
CC       42 sRNA (spf gene), it inhibits CRP-mediated gene activation when cells
CC       are grown in glucose, especially at higher than 30 degrees Celsius
CC       (when Spot 42 sRNA base pairing may be less efficient). Overexpression
CC       of this protein leads to decreased cell growth on non-glucose carbon
CC       sources, with particularly poor growth on galactose. Its overexpression
CC       blocks expression of some but not all CRP-dependent operons when grown
CC       on the appropriate sugar (blocks galactose- and maltose- but not
CC       sorbitol-specific operons), decreases the ability of CRP to activate
CC       transcription from the galE P1 promoter. Overexpression has no effect
CC       on CRP levels. The base-pairing activity of Spot 42 and the protein-
CC       coding activity of SpfP can be genetically separated.
